Spaghetti Cacio e Pepe

Spaghetti Cacio e Pepe is a classic Italian pasta dish that combines the simplicity of ingredients with bold flavors. Made with just a few key ingredients, this dish is a testament to the power of quality ingredients and the art of cooking. Here's a simple recipe to help you recreate this delightful meal at home.

Ingredients

  • 500 grams (1 pound) spaghetti
  • 200 grams (7 ounces) grated Pecorino Romano cheese
  • 200 grams (7 ounces) grated Parmesan cheese
  • 200 grams (7 ounces) black pepper, coarsely ground
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil
  • Salt, to taste

Steps

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil.
  2. Add the spaghetti to the boiling water and cook according to package instructions, al dente.
  3. While the pasta is cooking, combine the grated Pecorino Romano and Parmesan cheeses in a large bowl.
  4. Add the coarsely ground black pepper to the cheese mixture and mix well.
  5. In a separate pan, heat the extra-virgin olive oil over medium heat.
  6. Add the minced garlic to the pan and saut√© for about 30 seconds to release the flavor, being careful not to burn the garlic.
  7. Drain the cooked spaghetti and reserve some of the pasta water.
  8. Add the drained spaghetti to the pan with the garlic and olive oil.
  9. Toss the spaghetti with the garlic and oil to coat evenly.
  10. Quickly add the cheese mixture to the pan with the spaghetti, tossing gently to combine.
  11. If the sauce seems too thick, add a little of the reserved pasta water to create the desired consistency.
  12. Season with salt to taste.
  13. Serve the Spaghetti Cacio e Pepe immediately, garnished with additional black pepper if desired.
